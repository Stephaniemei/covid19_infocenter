About COVID-19




What is covid-19?

A coronavirus is a type of virus that causes infections in the nose, sinuses, and upper throat. The majority of coronaviruses are not dangerous.

At the beginning of 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) has identified SARS-CoV-2, after its outbreak in China in December 2019 as a new coronavirus type. The outbreak spread rapidly across the globe.

COVID-19 is a SARS-CoV-2 disease that can trigger what doctors refer to as an infection of respiratory tract. It may affect either your sinuses, nose, throat or windpipe and lungs.

It spreads like other coronaviruses, primarily through direct contact between people.Infections may be mild or fatal.

SARS-CoV-2 is one of seven coronavirus types, including those that cause severe diseases such as Middle East respiratory syndrome (MERS) and sudden acute respiratory syndrome (SARS) (SARS). The other coronaviruses are responsible for the majority of the colds we get during the year, but they aren't a significant threat to otherwise safe people.

The symptoms of COVID-19 infection

It is said that if the symptoms or combination of symptoms are present, you may have coronavirus:

  • Cough.
  • Diarrhea.
  • Tiredness.
  • Headaches.
  • Sore throat.
  • Fever or chills.
  • Nausea or vomiting.
  • Muscle or body aches.
  • Congestion or runny nose.
  • New loss of taste or smell.
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing.

Pneumonia, respiratory failure, heart problems, headache problems, septic shock and death can be caused by the virus. A disease called cytokine release syndrome or cytokine storm can cause numerous COVID-19 complications. This is the case when an inflammatory protein called cytokines triggers your immune system in order to flood the bloodstream.

How soon will a person develop symptoms after they are infected with COVID-19?

The period from infection to symptoms may vary from two to 14 days. This is called the "incubation period".

Before symptoms, the average time is five days.Symptoms can be mild to severe. COVID-19 causes only mild symptoms in approximately 80% of patients.



How is COVID-19 spreading from individual to individual?

COVID-19 is most likely to be spread:

  • If an infected person is coughing, sneezing, talking, singing, or breathing near you within six feet.
  • From close contact with an infected person such as touching, shaking hands.
  • By touching your eyes, mouth, or nose after touching surfaces where the virus has landed before washing your hands.


What are the origins of coronaviruses?

Coronaviruses are frequently discovered in bats, cats, and camels. The viruses live in the animals but do not infect them. These viruses can then spread to other animal species. Viruses may change (mutate) as they spread to new species. Eventually, the virus will be able to jump between animal species and infect humans. In the case of COVID-19, the first people infected in Wuhan, China, are thought to have contracted the virus at a food market selling meat, fish, and live animals – but the investigation is ongoing. Although researchers are unsure how people became infected, they do know that the virus can be transmitted directly from person to person via close contact.
